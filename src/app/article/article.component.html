One hundred days of code

Thoughts and more

Posted by Rodrigo Normando on December 3rd, 2018

Well, it's almost the beginning of December and I'd like to make some promises before the month of hard-to-keep promises finally arrives. These will be different, I promise.

Some days ago I discovered a challenge called '100 days of code' which basically consists of coding a minimum of one hour a day for the next 100 days. It seems tough but it was the push I needed to stop procrastinating. So I'll start that tomorrow.

I am into the tech subject for over two years now and into the tech industry for something like 14 months. So I'm not that experienced and also not that inexperienced anymore, I have taken this challenge mostly because I have a lot of interesting projects that I'd like to see going live, but, for now, are only ideas in my head. So the main reason I am writing this article is to state where I want to be 100 days from now, what are my projects and to invite you to come check it out now and then during the upcoming months.

Projects

An app and podcast for those who want to learn Portuguese:

This is a project I have with my fiance, she is a very good translator and has tons of experience in teaching Portuguese for non Portuguese speakers. If you want to learn Portuguese there's an app on your way, we intend to launch it at least in February on Android. If you already know Portuguese this app could also be useful, in the podcast there's gonna be plenty of cultural information.

Create a portfolio of Pure CSS Images:

I few days ago I didn't even know this kind of art could even exist, now I'm in love with it. Create images only with CSS - what a great idea!

CSS, the styling language of the web (and beyond), has helped web developers create better and better interfaces for their projects and it can also be used to create a more conventional way of art: illustrations. I love drawing since I was a kid and technology since I was a teenager, putting together two things I love feels like heaven. I will be doing a lot of CSS art in the upcoming months. For now I will let you with two of my drawings: one with pencil and one with code.
